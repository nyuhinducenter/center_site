Here are some links to other sites that we have found informative. A link here is not necessarily an endorsement of the materials and opinions found on these sites. Remember that Hinduism is a conglomeration of a wide variety of traditions, lineages, sects, etc. It has no central authority, and is not monolithic. Assertions made by a particular sect or lineage may not apply universally.

Abhyasa Ashram

" The goal of our sadhana or practices is the highest Joy that comes from the Realization in direct experience of the center of consciousness, the Self, the Atman or Purusha, which is one and the same with the Absolute Reality. This Self-Realization comes through the three streams of: 1) traditional Yoga meditation of the Yoga Sutras, 2) the contemplative insight of Advaita Vedanta, and 3) the intense devotion of Samaya Sri Vidya Tantra, the three of which complement one another like fingers on a hand. - Yoga - The goal of Yoga is Yoga." Lots of information here.

American Institute of Vedic Studies

American Institute of Vedic Studies’ online courses provide you with a distinct opportunity to learn from Dr. David Frawley (Pandit Vamadeva Shastri) and begin an enduring journey with the timeless wisdom of the Vedas. It focuses on Ayurveda, Yoga-Vedanta, Vedic astrology and their interconnections. It correlates Veda and Tantra along with the background system of Hinduism or ‘Sanatana Dharma’. It emphasizes bringing together the greater Yoga tradition in an integral manner with regard to its Vedic origins. Aside from the courses, there are lots of resources on the site.

Drik Panchang: World Hindu Calendar

The drikPanchang.com site generates panchang, festival and vrat dates for more than 20,000 cities. All festivals and vrats are listed based on location. All calculations are based on Drik Ganita i.e. on precise calculations of planetary positions. Aside from calendar info there are sections on Jyotish, Music, Art, Etc. This is a fabulous resource.

The International Gita Society

The Bhagavad Gita is a well-respected religious book that strives to provide individuals with a feeling of unity and the spiritual guidance that all other religions aim to create. Though the original Bhagavad Gita is in Sanskrit, it has been transcribed into various other languages to give others the ability to read the Bhagavad-Gita teachings of Krishna. The International Gita Society allows you to buy Bhagavad Gita in English, as well as in several other languages, so you can enjoy the teachings of this book. PDFs are available free of charge.
  • Look for the "12 More Vedic Scriptures" link on their homepage. There you will find many of the major Hindu Scriptures available as free PDF files. (The site asks not to link to the page directly.)

Shiva Shakti Mandalam

Tantra, or more properly tantrika, is a diverse and rich spiritual tradition of the Indian sub-continent. Although in recent years, in the Western world, it has become almost exclusively associated with sex, in reality this is one aspect of what is a way of life.
